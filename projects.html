Shooting For the Stars With a Refreshing Upgrade

October 2024
FITS Phase

I was a part of Unique Emporium’s journey when they were just starting. I created the existing logo per the owners’ specifications. As time passed, I knew in my heart that I could do better. Also, having been on the company’s website and briefly discussing it with one of the owners, I was aware of the site’s need for a refresh!

I reached out to one of the owners and offered my services! Shortly after, I sat down and had a long conversation with him about what direction to take things. I gathered inspiration into a moodboard, and I researched a few items I wanted to implement.

SITS Phase

I sketched my initial wireframes on paper and my initial logo ideas in Adobe Fresco. I took my wireframes to Adobe XD and shared my rough ideas with some peers. After those good conversations, I made some decisions and took the logo into Adobe Illustrator. I compiled the beginnings of a brand: logos, color palette, typefaces, and vibe. After I created some mockups of the website, I gathered more feedback from peers. I refined my mockups before finally diving into some code!

When I create a website, I follow the mobile-first approach, and I work page by page. Not much changed from my mockup 2.0 to the finished site. The Events page was the greatest challenge, so I made a practice CodePen to get the functionality down. Along the way, I did teach myself a few new JavaScript and CSS tricks.

When I look at the completed website, I am filled with pride. It was a long journey, and I made it! Seeing the website and “rebrand” through was important because I believed in myself.

Results

A stellar new logo and some brand guidelines. A fully-coded, 4-page website utilizing HTML, CSS, & JavaScript adhering to said guidelines. The website is fun, energetic, and filled with well-structured important information.

Unique Emporium

Background

Unique Emporium is a trading card game (TCG) retail shop located in Saint Louis, MO. Opening their doors in the fall of 2022, owners Dave and Arif set out to build more than a TCG retail shop; they wanted to build a fun, inviting community for players of all skill levels.

Challenge

As a young company, Unique Emporium lacks a strong, cohesive brand identity. The current company website is poorly designed and ineffectively structured. It was built with a do-it-yourself platform by someone who self-admits they did not know what they were doing and felt limited by the builder’s features. The website does not convey the fun, welcoming business Unique Emporium works hard to be.

Night or Day, Poké Mart Has You Covered

October 2023
FITS Phase

Poké Mart reps told me they wanted to have a dark and light mode-compatible single-page website so customers could purchase all their top-sellers from each category. I started by seeking inspiration and creating a moodboard.

SITS Phase

Per typical Janessa, I began with some sketches on paper. I asked a few peers for feedback on my wireframe, then dove straight into the code: HTML, CSS, then JavaScript. As I worked on this webpage, I saved versions to refer back to. The biggest challenge here was JavaScript. I was still pretty new to it at the time.

Results

Poké Mart now has a fully coded web page for trainers to order their key items at any time of day or night, send in questions or comments, and even choose a dark or light mode. Deliveries are booming!

Poké Mart

Background

A Poké Mart, short for Pokémon Mart, is a convenience store found strategically placed in every city in each of the nine Pokémon regions. Trainers can visit a Poké Mart to stock up on necessary supplies to support them on their journey to becoming Pokémon Masters. The exact merchandise available varies based on the location and trainer’s level.

Challenge

Sometimes trainers just can’t make it to a physical Poké Mart store because they are too busy battling their Pokémon. Customers still need Poké balls, healing items, and evolution items even when they are on the go. Poké Mart wants to take their go-to stores online to allow these dedicated trainers a way to order delivery services.

Interrogating My Migraine

August 2023
FITS Phase

I began by asking myself questions and writing down the migraine knowledge I had. Then I conducted some research to fact-check myself and gain any new insights. I formed the questions much like the ones I already asked myself, and wrote the possible answers. Next I looked up inspiration and created a moodboard of ideas.

SITS Phase

It was time to get started, so I drew some pencil sketches of potential styles and layouts. I mapped the questions. Then I created a storyboard of all the landing screens I needed to create. I drew the organic shape backgrounds in Adobe Fresco and then imported them into Adobe XD. I then added the “buttons” and text, and I prototyped the connections. Then came testing and finishing touches.

Results

I had a functioning prototype of a very helpful migraine app. When I used it I knew that I was not skipping any questions that I need to consider when I’m getting a headache. Maybe one day I’ll develop it further and put it in the app store!

Migraine App

Background

Migraine is more than a headache; it’s a debilitating neurological disease. Managing triggers, symptoms, and various treatments can be an ever exhausting endeavor. I have suffered from this condition since I was a child. After all these years, each headache is still accompanied by the hope that it’s just a headache.

Challenge

I must act fast to determine which method of treatment I need to reduce the chances of the pain ruining my day. Each time I feel a bit of pain, I go through this same series of questions in my head. The problem with that is my head hurts, so I’m likely to forget some of the steps. I wanted to create an app that would make this mental pop quiz a little less stressful.

Turning the Page for UX

April 2023
FITS Phase

A group of peers and I conducted a SWOT analysis and found the need for improved technology integration. One of the ways I wanted to do this was by focusing on their mobile application. I wrote a creative brief identifying my goal, target audience, tone, competitors, etc. I knew I wanted to demonstrate an added feature.

SITS Phase

I created a storyboard for my GIF. At first to just get a feel for what the animation would look like, but I made a second version with more details afterward. From my storyboards, I began working until I had 9 iterations and a final product.

Results

A demonstrative GIF of a potential change Barnes & Noble could make to their app to engage more people and improve customer relationships. The GIF gives shareholders the opportunity to understand the proposed interface with an improved user experience!

Barnes & Noble

Background

Tracing its roots to 1873, B&N has grown to be a consistent and reliable leader in book sales. The book retailer is best known for its welcoming stores offering a plethora of books and other media as well as a Starbucks cafe.

In 1997, Barnes & Noble launched the company’s eCommerce site. They optimized their website for mobile phones in May 2008, and due to its success the company launched a new mobile application in the summer of 2022.

Challenge

A thorough SWOT analysis identified an overarching problem of B&N not keeping up with the modern world. The mobile application with a 2.2-star rating on the app store contributed to this issue. Most of the complaints about the app are due to poor user experience.

A mobile app should be distinct from the company’s mobile website and offer new useful additional features to users. With Barnes & Noble having a foot in both brick-and-mortar and eCommerce, it makes sense for the company’s mobile application to integrate the two. At the time of research, no competitor offered this, so this opportunity gives B&N a unique advantage.

Typography for Social Change

July 2023
FITS Phase

This project started with A LOT of research. I needed the facts! From there, I identified overarching topics. I made some infographic-inspired moodboards and came up with stylistic themes for each series I would create.

SITS Phase

For sketches, I had the creative idea to use sticky notes! I knew I would be utilizing Instagram and needed perfect squares. After completing my sketches, I jumped into Photoshop and got to work.

Results

Three strategic Instagram series that dissect the complexities of student debt through visually compelling infographics. Each series tackles a distinct aspect of the crisis with thoughtful design and clear messaging empowering viewers to understand, discuss, and act!

Cancel Student Debt

Background

Social media often gets a bad rap, but platforms can be a very powerful tool. Posting images on Instagram, for example, can empower people and start a movement. People can reach each other across the globe, raise their voices, and create awareness for the causes they care most about. One such cause is the student debt crisis in America which impacts over 43 million individuals.

Challenge

America has a combined $1.75 trillion in student debt. Rising tuition costs, stagnant wages, and complex loan structures have created a systemic problem that threatens individuals & broader economic mobility. Many people cannot afford their payments. Financial strain delays marriage and having a family. Some even miss out on owning a reliable car or purchasing a home. We need to leverage social media to raise awareness, educate the public, and push for policy reform.
